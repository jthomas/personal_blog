Cooking With the Alpkit BruPot

Feb 16th, 2015 9:11 pm

Alpkit’s BruPot got a sustained “field test” on my recent wild camp, combined with the Vango Compact Stove and Primus’ Wind-shield. Anodised titanium cooking pot, one litre capacity, with heat exchanger on the base to maximise heating efficiency. Handles wrap round the pot during storage. Rubber lid, with drinking hole, along with an insulating cover for the sides. Marks on the inside for 250 millimetres increments make measuring water easy.

Pot sold standalone (£20) or with a stove in their BruKit package (£35).

Using in the evening and morning, boiling water for my meals and coffee, with no issues. Boiling time somewhere between five and ten minutes, wasn’t paying particular attention, despite the very cold temperatures.

Primus’ Wind-shield was another great addition to my micro-cooking setup. Slots over the gas cannister when not in use, which can both be stowed inside the BruPot.

Unfortunately, mini-stove is marginally too large to fit inside the BruPot with the cannister and windscreen. This extra space can be handily used for lighters, fire stick or coffee sachets.

This micro-cooking setup has now replaced the flask in my rucksack. Having a stove allows you to make multiple brews on a trip and even hot lunches. Weight and space differences compared to a flask seem negligible.

Extremely impressed with the BruPot. Buying outdoors gear is normally choosing two from cheap, light and robust. Alpkit’s BruPot has all three.
